Section 01 · Market Thesis

Why now.

The market is telling us two things at the same time. Both matter. Most investors are only listening to one of them.

The first thing the market is saying: asset prices are stretched. The Buffett Indicator, which compares total stock market value to U.S. GDP, sat at 230% as of December 31, 2025 and 227% in April 2026. Warren Buffett historically called readings approaching 200% a sign of "playing with fire." The Shiller CAPE ratio, which smooths earnings over a 10-year window, was at 36.48 in April 2026 — territory only seen before the dot-com peak and the 2021 bubble.

The second thing the market is saying: portfolio balances look strong. The S&P 500 rose 211.3% from May 2016 to March 2026. Investors see rising 401k statements and feel rich. Most do not realize that M2 money supply rose 77.2% over the same period, meaning a meaningful portion of that gain came from monetary expansion, not durable value creation.

The collision

Investors feel wealthy because their balances have grown. The growth has been real in nominal terms. But the same investors are exposed to elevated valuations, monetary distortion, and the assumption that public-market liquidity equals safety. Liquidity is not protection. It just means you can sell quickly.

State Street's 2026 alternatives outlook described stock markets near all-time highs, elevated valuations of risky assets, record concentration within key equity indices, and high equity-bond correlations. Institutional capital is already moving. Family offices are increasing alternatives allocations. The retail investor has not caught up yet.

The strategic question

If a portion of portfolio growth is tied to market liquidity and elevated valuations, what portion of capital should be moved into documented commerce?

This is the question Totem is positioned to answer. Not as a competing investment. As a different category of capital deployment entirely.

Why this is the moment

Three conditions are converging that make this the right time to define a new category, not later:

01
Macro fragility. Investors are starting to question whether their balances reflect strength or liquidity.
02
Alternatives shift. Institutional capital is rotating toward documented, contracted, productive deployment.
03
Track record. Totem has 2,000+ completed transactions and 0.00% defaults. The proof exists. The story does not yet.

The work in this document is the strategic answer to the question above. We are not building a better CD or a better bond. We are building the first place where capital can be deployed into productive U.S. commerce with both principal and returns insured by the largest trade credit insurer in the world.

That asset class did not exist before Totem. It is the reason prospects are skeptical. They are not skeptical of us specifically. They are skeptical of a category they have never heard of. The job over the next 12 months is to make the category real in their minds.

The reason no one knows about this is because nothing exists with this type of asset class. We are building a brand new one. — JJ, on the foundational insight

Section 02 · Strategic Reframe

Totem is not an alternative investment.

That sentence is the entire strategy. Once partners internalize it, every other decision in this document follows. Once prospects internalize it, every objection collapses.

The problem with how we have been positioning

When we say "alternative investment," we put ourselves in a category investors already have a model for. That model includes hedge funds, private equity, REITs, commodity ETFs, and crypto. All of those compete on yield, complexity, accreditation, and risk tolerance. None of them win the conversation we are actually trying to have.

The conversation we are trying to have is not "should you replace your hedge fund with Totem." The conversation is "why is your CD, your 401k, your stocks, and your real estate uninsured against loss of either principal or return."

The category we are actually building

The category claim

Totem is the only place, that we know of, where capital can be deployed into productive U.S. commerce with 100% of principal AND 100% of returns insured by Allianz.

Not principal alone. Not returns alone. Both. Backed by the largest trade credit insurance company in the world. Tied to verified Fortune 100 purchase orders. Repaid on a 90 to 100 day cycle. Verifiable on the phone with our partners before any capital moves.

What this category replaces

This is the part we have been getting wrong. We have been competing for the 5% of capital that is actively shopping for short-cycle PO finance. That market is small. Most of it does not exist.

The actual market is 100% of the capital currently sitting in CDs, 401ks, brokerage accounts, savings accounts, real estate equity, and idle cash. The job is to convince capital to leave its current category and move into ours.

That is a much bigger market. It is also a much harder sale. Investors are scared of the unfamiliar. They are not wrong to be. The job is not to overpower their skepticism. The job is to walk them through the structure, name our buyers, hand them the phone with our partners, and let them verify it themselves.

The 100% insurance question

This is the single sentence that makes the case stronger than any spreadsheet. We will use it as the test against every option in Section 04:

The Question
When was the last time your 401k, your CDs, your stocks, or your real estate insured 100% of your principal AND 100% of your returns?
The answer for every option except Totem is the same word. Never.

What we name the category

The language we have been using is "purchase order finance" or "PO funding." Both are accurate. Neither is sticky.

The language we will use going forward is commerce-backed private funding. Three reasons:

01

It tells the story instantly

"Commerce-backed" tells the prospect that real business activity is happening behind their capital. It is not synthetic. It is not market sentiment. It is goods moving to buyers.

02

It stakes new ground

"Private funding" sits next to private credit and private equity in the prospect's mind, but the "commerce-backed" qualifier separates us. We are not generic alternatives. We are specifically tied to commerce.

03

It is defensible in compliance

The language is descriptive, not promotional. It avoids "investment," "guaranteed," and "risk-free" while still landing the punch. The structure stays compliant. The story stays sharp.

We are not trying to build a better version of something that already exists. We are trying to make people stop and ask: how did I never hear about this before?

Daily liquidity (vs. public stocks)

Totem capital is committed for 90 to 100 days. Stocks can be sold in seconds. The reframe: 90 days is shorter than most investors actually hold their positions. Liquidity matters less than protection at exit.

LOSS 02

FDIC clarity (vs. CDs)

FDIC is a household acronym. Allianz trade credit insurance is not. The reframe: FDIC covers principal up to $250K at 4% returns. Allianz covers principal AND returns at 5% per cycle. The acronym is unfamiliar. The economics are not close.

LOSS 03

Tax-deferred compounding (vs. retirement)

401k contributions are pre-tax. Totem returns are taxable. The reframe: retirement and Totem are not the same bucket. The 401k builds the long-term wrapper. Totem builds productive capital. Self-directed IRAs bridge the two.

Public-market familiarity

Investors have been told for 50 years that the S&P 500 is the safe choice. The reframe: familiarity is not protection. The Buffett Indicator at 230% is a familiar market at unfamiliar valuations. The work in this 12-month build is to make Totem feel familiar through repetition, story, and verification.

Section 05 · Wealth Preservation Blueprint

The framework we put on every prospect's desk.

The Wealth Preservation Blueprint is the branded scorecard prospects use to evaluate any capital option. Five questions. Same framework. Every category answers them. Most fail. Totem does not.

Why a framework, not a pitch

Prospects do not buy from people who pitch. Prospects buy from people who teach them how to think. The Wealth Preservation Blueprint is the teaching tool. The job of every Totem prospect-facing asset is to leave the reader with a framework they can use even if they never buy.

That is the long game. Investors who use the Blueprint will eventually compare every option they hold against it. Most options fail the test. Totem is the only option, that we know of, that passes all five.

01

What creates the return?

Most investments answer this with abstraction: "the market," "the economy," "growth." Totem answers it with specificity: a Fortune 100 buyer paid a verified vendor for goods we financed. The 5% origination fee is the contractual payment for that role.

02

What protects the principal?

FDIC, SIPC, and U.S. government backing are familiar but limited. They cover specific scenarios, not investment performance. Totem stacks Allianz trade credit insurance, a first-position performance guarantee equal to 100% of principal, and joint account controls that earmark capital to a single transaction.

03

What protects the return?

This is the question almost no other investment can answer. Stocks have no protected return. CDs cap returns at deposit terms. Annuities have caps and surrender penalties. Totem insures the 5% origination fee under the same Allianz structure that protects principal. Both legs covered.

04

How long is capital committed?

Liquidity is not protection, but committed capital that you cannot recover at need is a real cost. Totem's 90 to 100 day cycle is shorter than every CD term, every retirement lockup, and every private fund. Capital returns on a known clock.

05

Can the counterparty be verified?

This is the close. Totem invites verification. We will put any qualified prospect on the phone with our partners to walk through the buyer relationship, the vendor history, the insurance policy, the joint account structure, and the repayment path. No other investment in the universe ends with that offer.
